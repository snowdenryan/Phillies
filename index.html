How The Phillies Went From Worst To First

By Ryan Snowden

Tuesday, March 8, 2016

The Philadelphia Phillies haven't exactly been a model franchise for professional sports. After their 'glory days' in the late 70's and early 80's, the team entered a dismal state of losing. From 1986 to 2000, and only once in that span were they able to finish with more wins than losses. It took them ten years, but after receiving the first overall selection in the 1998 MLB Draft, the Phillies were able to build a team Philadelphians would remember forever.

In 1997, the Phillies finished with the worst record in baseball and clinched the first pick in the draft for the following year.
With the first pick in the 1998 MLB Draft, the Phillies selected outfielder Pat Burrell, who remained with the team for ten years.
Fans realize it might be a while before the Phils put together some winning seasons. The team finished in 3rd place in the division with a record of 77-85.
The turn of the century proved to be a backtrack for the Phillies as they finish in last place in the National League East with a record of 65-97, their worst record since 1969.
First year manager and former World Series Champion with the Phillies Lary Bowa was awarded National League Manager of the Year Award after he lead the team to their first winning season since their 1993 World Series appearance. The team finished 86-76, and were excited for the years to come.
Of course every rebuilding might have some bumps in the road. The team won 80 games and lost 81. Thats about as mediocre as you can get.
The final year in Veteran's Stadium began a few years of consistency for the Phillies. Jim Thome lead the league in home runs with 47, and the team finished at 86-76. They still weren't ready for the Playoffs, but were forced to watch their division rival Florida Marlins win their 2nd World Series in only their 10th year in existence. Philadelphia was still stuck on 1 title in 100 years.
Another 86-win season and no playoff appearance for Philadelphia. Citizen's Bank Park's first decade was an era of prosperity. Newcomers Ryan Howard and Chase Utley joined Jimmy Rollins as the 'Big 3' in the Phillies infield.
Charlie Manuel joined the team as their new manager and lead them to 88 wins. What was officially Ryan Howard's rookie year, proved to be the best year among rookies. He brought home the NL Rookie of the Year Award, while outfielder Bobby Abreu won the Home Run Derby.
The time was coming. Everyone could feel it. The Atlanta Braves 14-year streak of winning the National League East had come to an end. Ryan Howard evolved quickly to the best player in the league, bringing home MVP honors with 58 home runs. The Phillies only missed the playoffs by three games, and they were only getting better.
The year of the milestone included Barry Bonds breaking the career home run record, and the Phillies completing the largest comeback for a division title in MLB history. With 17 games remaining in September, the Phillies trailed the Mets by 7.5 games and ended up winning it by one game on the final day of the season. The Phillies had finally made the playoffs for the first time since 1993! The season ended in a first round exit, but fans were still ecstatic after all these years.
PHINALLY. Fans have waited since 1983 to see a championship in any sport in Philadelphia. On Oct. 29, 2008, Pat Burrell played his final game in City of Brotherly Love and the Philadelphia Phillies won the previously suspended Game 5 of the World Series against the Tampa Bay Rays, to clinch the series 4-1. Two days later on Halloween, the Phils and 2,000,000 fans (myself included) celebrated all the way down Broad Street for a day they would never forget.
